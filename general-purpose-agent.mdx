## What is the General Purpose Research Agent?

The General Purpose Research Agent is Paperguide's flexible, all-in-one assistant for broad and custom research workflows. It works across discovery, analysis, synthesis, and writing so you do not need to switch tools for every question.

Unlike specialized agents such as [AI Search 2.0](/ai-search-new), [Literature Review](/literature-review), or [Chat with PDF 2.0](/chat-with-pdf-new), this agent is optimized for mixed, open-ended tasks that cut across multiple steps in your research process.

You can also learn more about the agent on the product page: https://paperguide.ai/general-purpose-agent/

## What it can help with

Use the General Purpose Research Agent when you want one assistant to stay with you from early questions to drafted text. It is especially useful for:

- Ask research questions and get evidence-backed answers  
- Find relevant papers for any research topic  
- Compare papers, studies, and key findings  
- Summarize papers into clear key insights  
- Identify research gaps and study limitations  
- Edit and refine academic or research drafts  

Beyond these, the agent can plan search strategies, draft screening or extraction plans, and extract structured details such as study design, population, interventions or variables, outcomes, limitations, and key findings.

<Callout kind="info">

Always review the agent's outputs, check cited papers directly, and apply your own expert judgment. Use the agent to support your research, not to replace critical appraisal or manual verification.

</Callout>

## When to use this agent vs other agents

Use the General Purpose Research Agent as your default when your work spans multiple stages or you are not sure which specialized tool fits yet.

### Choose the General Purpose agent when

- You want to **explore a new topic** and refine your questions over time.  
- You need to **move back and forth** between finding papers, summarizing them, and drafting text.  
- You are **brainstorming methods, variables, or workflows** for a project.  
- You want to **iterate on drafts** with context from papers and your own notes.

### Prefer a dedicated agent when

- You need **targeted paper search** over large databases with filters and advanced ranking → use [AI Search 2.0](/ai-search-new).  
- You are building a **systematic or structured literature review** with workbooks and extraction → use [Literature Review](/literature-review).  
- You want to **deeply interrogate specific PDFs or reference folders** → use [Chat with PDF 2.0](/chat-with-pdf-new) or [Chat with PDF](/chat-with-pdf).  
- You are primarily **writing or polishing a document** with structured sections and citation tools → use [AI Writer](/ai-writer/overview).

## How it differs from other agents

Use this agent alongside specialized tools rather than instead of them. The tabs below summarize the main differences.

<Tabs>

<Tab title="General Purpose" icon="zap">

The General Purpose Research Agent is designed to be your **all-round research assistant**.

- Works well for **open-ended, exploratory questions**.
- Stays useful as you move from **idea → search → analysis → writing**.
- Adapts to **custom workflows**, including planning search strategies or data extraction templates.
- Handles **mixed inputs**, such as high-level questions, specific papers, and your own notes.

Use it when you want flexibility and continuity more than strict structure.

</Tab>

<Tab title="AI Search 2.0" icon="search">

[AI Search 2.0](/ai-search-new) specializes in **finding relevant papers** from public research databases.

- Best for **high-quality discovery** on a focused query.  
- Provides **search controls and filters** that you can tune.  
- Ideal as a **first step** before deeper review or analysis.

Use AI Search 2.0 when your main goal is to **find the right studies** efficiently.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Literature Review" icon="book-open">

[Literature Review](/literature-review) is built for **structured reviewing at scale**.

- Organizes work into **workbooks** for repeatable reviews.  
- Helps with **screening, tagging, and extracting data** across many papers.  
- Suits **systematic or scoping reviews** where process and traceability matter.

Use Literature Review when you need a **rigorous, repeatable workflow** for many papers.

</Tab>

<Tab title="Chat with PDF/Papers" icon="file">

[Chat with PDF 2.0](/chat-with-pdf-new) and [Chat with PDF](/chat-with-pdf) focus on **interacting with specific documents**.

- Optimized for **question-answering and summarizing** a given PDF or set of papers.  
- Great for **close reading**, extracting passages, and clarifying methods or results.  
- Ideal when the key question is **"What does this paper say?"**.

Use Chat with PDF/Papers when you want to **zoom in** on particular documents.

</Tab>

<Tab title="AI Writer" icon="edit">

[AI Writer](/ai-writer/overview) focuses on **producing and refining written documents**.

- Helps generate **outlines, sections, and drafts**.  
- Integrates **citations and references** from your library.  
- Supports **Ask AI** commands for iterative editing.

Use AI Writer when your main task is **writing or editing** with research as supporting context.

</Tab>

</Tabs>

## How to use the General Purpose Research Agent

Use the General Purpose Research Agent from the agent chooser in Paperguide whenever you want a single assistant to stay with you through a task.

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the General Purpose agent" icon="play-circle">

- Go to the agent or workspace area where you normally start research sessions.  
- Select **General Purpose Research Agent** from the list of available agents.

You now have a chat session dedicated to broad research support.

</Step>

<Step title="Describe your goal and context" icon="edit">

- Start by stating your **research question, project type, and constraints** (for example, field, timeframe, population).  
- Add any **key papers, PDFs, or notes** you already have, or mention where you are in the process (exploring, reviewing, writing).

The agent will use this context to tailor search strategies, summaries, and suggestions.

</Step>

<Step title="Iterate across discovery, analysis, and writing" icon="refresh-ccw">

- Ask it to **find and explain relevant papers**, flagging key methods, populations, and outcomes.  
- Request **comparisons, gap analysis, and limitations** based on the cited studies.  
- Move into **drafting or refining text**, such as introductions, related work sections, or discussion points.

Switch between these tasks in the same conversation so the agent keeps track of your evolving understanding.

</Step>

<Step title="Save and move work into specialized tools" icon="folder">

- When you reach a more focused phase, **move to dedicated agents**:
  - Send specific questions or PDFs to **Chat with PDF 2.0**.  
  - Use **AI Search 2.0** for deeper discovery on narrowed queries.  
  - Bring structured insights into **Literature Review** workbooks or **AI Writer** documents.

This workflow lets the General Purpose agent guide your thinking, while specialized tools handle the heavy lifting for each stage.

</Step>

</Steps>
