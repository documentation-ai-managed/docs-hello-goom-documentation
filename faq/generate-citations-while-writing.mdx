---
title: How do I generate citations while writing?
---
With Paperguide’s built-in Reference Manager, you can add citations for your references directly while writing and even cite papers from public research databases.

To generate in-text citations, highlight any section of text and click the **Cite** button to open our Citation Manager.

<Callout kind="tip" collapsed="false">
  You can also use the "**@**" keyboard shortcut to open our Citation Manager.
</Callout>

- Place the cursor where you want to cite on the **Cite** button on the toolbar (or) type “**@.”**

  1. Then, a citation manager dialog opens.

  2. It includes two tabs: **My References** and **Research Database**

### Cite Papers from References

- My References tab includes the papers stored in your reference manager.

- Search the paper from your references that you need to cite.

- Then, click on **the + Cite** button.

### Search & Cite Papers from Public Research Database:

- You can search papers from the public database from this tab.

- Search the paper by title in the research database that you need to cite. And click on **the + Cite** button.
